# Example worldbuilding text for testing the auto-scanner

In the year 1201, the Chaos Wars began. King Joshua Fester of Calmone gathered his armies at the capital city of Sarsda, preparing for the invasion from the north. Queen Anna stood beside him, her wisdom guiding the Council of Lords through these dark times.

The Kingdom of Calmone, located on the eastern shores of the continent Elise, had long been protected by the Whiteface Mountains. But now, dark forces from the mysterious land of Eden threatened to break through.

Lord Conner, the King's most trusted advisor, spoke at the war council: "We must fortify Dustow, the merchant port. If it falls, our trade routes will be severed." Swordmaster Keshner nodded in agreement, his hand resting on the pommel of his legendary blade.

The Age of Magic had ended centuries ago, during the Forgotten Age (circa year 100), when the Tausin Empire poisoned the world's magic. Now, only whispers of the ancient arcane arts remained. Some said that in the elven kingdom of Verlone, beyond the mountains, wizards still practiced their craft in secret.

Marshall Jennings commanded the northern garrison, watching for any sign of movement from the enemy. He had fought in many battles, but this war felt different. The prophecies spoke of a great darkness that would consume the land unless a hero arose.

In the forests south of Sarsda, a young ranger named Tari Alcorin discovered ancient ruins. She found inscriptions speaking of the War of The White Council (320-400), a conflict that had reshaped the very geography of the continent. The texts mentioned powerful artifacts hidden throughout the realm.

Meanwhile, the mysterious Ravage, an immortal being said to be 800 years old, emerged from the shadows. His motives were unclear, but legends claimed he had witnessed the rise and fall of empires. Some believed him to be a guardian, others feared him as a harbinger of doom.

The Third Age was upon them, beginning in the year 3000. Would the kingdoms of humanity survive, or would they fall like those before them? Only time would tell, as the threads of fate wove together politics, magic, war, and ancient prophecies into a tapestry of epic proportions.
